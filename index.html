In 2016, the White House awarded Youth Policy Institute $4 million to expand tech access to the diverse communities of Los Angeles. Through an innovative job training program, TechHire prepares individuals for high-demand careers in technology requiring no degree.

Since then, we've connected employers to job seekers who possess the skills and the drive to succeed in the industry. We specialize in bridging candidates who've taken unconventional paths to acquiring the requisite skills to reputable employers filling the digital divide in Los Angeles.

TechHireLA is on the bleeding edge of Tech Access in three key ways:

CONNECTING TO NEW OPPORTUNITIES
TechHireLA is not a recruiter firm but has the capability of providing job placement assistance and provide unprecedented support from state, local, and non-profit agencies to those who qualify.
Learn More
PROVIDING TECH EDUCATION AND DEVELOPMENT
TechHireLA is partnered with Sabio.LA and Firehose Project to provide training to scholarship recipients and concurrently be trained in house by our specialists on professional and personal development.

Youth Policy Institute (YPI)’s TechHire project offers services and programs throughout the community in which no qualified participant shall be excluded or otherwise subject to discrimination on the basis of race, color, national origin, age, sex, religion, political beliefs or disability. This organization is an equal opportunity provider. Additionally, YPI has been awarded a $4 million grant to enhance opportunities within the technology sector which represents 78% of the project’s funding from the U.S. Department of Labor’s Employment and Training administration. The remaining $1.1 million dollars which is 12% of the budget is to be financed by private and public sources.

Esta organizacion es proveedor de oportunidades de igualdad.
